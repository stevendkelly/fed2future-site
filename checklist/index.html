The Federal Exit Checklist

Everything to lock down before and after your last day in federal service — the career steps and the money steps, in order.

90+ days out (or the day you get RIF notice)

  • ✓Download your eOPF, all SF-50s, last 3 SF-52s, and your most recent performance appraisals — you lose easy access at separation.
  • ✓Verify your creditable service and MRA date; check whether weeks of timing change your FERS options (see the deferred vs. postponed guide).
  • ✓Get current TSP, FEGLI, and FEHB statements; note your TSP loan balance if any.
  • ✓Translate your series with the Skills Crosswalk and build your 2-page private-sector resume.
  • ✓Quietly update LinkedIn — headline in civilian language, not your position description.

60 days out

  • ✓Price your health-coverage bridge: compare Temporary Continuation of Coverage (~102% of premium), a spouse's plan, and marketplace plans.
  • ✓Map severance and lump-sum annual leave payouts, and set aside taxes — both are taxable income, often withheld light.
  • ✓Apply to 5–10 translated roles via the State Job Board and your private-sector targets; ask three colleagues for recommendations while you still share an office.
  • ✓If clearance-eligible: note your clearance type and investigation date — it stays current for about 2 years and is a paid asset with contractors.

30 days out

  • ✓Resolve or plan your TSP loan; decide nothing about moving TSP money yet (see the TSP guide).
  • ✓Confirm your unused annual leave balance (paid out) vs. sick leave (not paid, but restored if you return to federal service).
  • ✓Get personal copies of anything you're allowed to keep: training certificates, awards, writing samples cleared for release.
  • ✓Set up a personal email for references and job accounts — your .gov address dies on day one.

After separation

  • ✓File for unemployment (UCFE) if involuntarily separated — federal employees qualify through their state.
  • ✓Watch for your final paycheck, annual leave payout, and severance schedule; reconcile all three.
  • ✓Decide your TSP and FERS moves on your timeline, not the market's — nothing expires in week one.
  • ✓Keep networking: most former feds land through people, not portals.
