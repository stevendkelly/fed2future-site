Translating Your GS Pay to a Private-Sector Salary (Without Underselling)

The most expensive mistake separating feds make is anchoring on their GS base salary. Your federal compensation was bigger than your paycheck — and your market value is usually higher than your grade suggests. Do this math before you name a number.

Start with total compensation, not base pay

Your real federal comp included the FERS pension accrual (worth roughly 8–15% of salary depending on assumptions), the 5% TSP match, generous leave accrual, and FEHB premium sharing. A GS-13 at $115K base was earning something closer to $135K–$145K in total-compensation terms. That — not your base — is the floor for comparing offers.

Price the role, not your history

Private employers pay for the role's market rate, and for translated federal skills that rate is often above your GS band — especially in procurement, cybersecurity, compliance, and finance. Pull ranges from posted salaries (many states and several states' laws now require them), BLS occupational data, and levels-style comp sites for your target title and metro. Come to the table with the role's range, and never volunteer your federal salary first.

Value what you're gaining and losing

Moving to state government? Base pay often runs somewhat below federal, but many states still offer defined-benefit pensions and your federal experience may count toward seniority (Pennsylvania now credits it by executive order). Moving private? Expect higher base and bonus but weaker retirement — negotiate salary to cover the pension you're giving up, and check the 401(k) match and vesting schedule before you compare offers.

Negotiate like it's a procurement

You've watched vendors negotiate with the government for years — use the same playbook. Get the offer in writing, evaluate against your requirements (total comp floor), counter once with data, and be willing to trade: signing bonus for base, extra PTO for salary, remote days for commute costs. The first offer is the vendor's opening bid, not the ceiling.

The short version

  • ✓Do compute your federal total comp before you set a salary floor.
  • ✓Do research posted ranges for the target title in the target metro — data beats hope.
  • ✓Do negotiate every offer at least once; the worst realistic outcome is 'no'.
  • ✗Don't anchor on GS base pay — you'll leave 15–25% on the table.
  • ✗Don't disclose your federal salary before they name a range.
  • ✗Don't compare offers on base alone; retirement and health benefits swing real value by thousands.
Educational, not advice: This guide explains general rules that change and have exceptions. Verify current figures with OPM, TSP.gov, and IRS guidance, and consult a licensed professional about your specific situation before acting.
