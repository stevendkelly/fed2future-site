Translating a Federal Resume for the Private Sector (and State Jobs)

Your federal resume was built to satisfy USAJOBS scoring — long, exhaustive, and full of series numbers and acronyms. Outside government, that document works against you. Recruiters spend under 10 seconds on the first pass. Here's the translation.

Cut it to two pages — ruthlessly

Drop the GS levels, series numbers, supervisor phone numbers, hours per week, and salary history. Nobody outside government knows what a GS-0343-13 is, and the details that USAJOBS required are noise to an applicant tracking system (ATS). What survives: your last 10–15 years, told through outcomes.

Translate the language, not just the titles

“Program analysis” becomes business analysis or process improvement. “Contracting officer's representative” becomes vendor management. “Budget formulation and execution” becomes FP&A and budget ownership. Start from the job posting you want: mirror its keywords honestly, because both the ATS and the human are matching against them.

Use the crosswalk on this site to find the private and state titles for your series — then write your resume in that vocabulary.

Lead every bullet with a result

Federal writing buries outcomes under process. Flip it: “Cut grant processing time 30% by redesigning intake workflow serving 5,000 applicants/year.” Number, action, scope. If a bullet has no number, ask yourself what changed because you were there — budget touched, people served, hours saved, findings closed, dollars recovered.

Prepare STAR stories for the interview

Private-sector and state interviews run on behavioral questions. Prepare six stories in Situation-Task-Action-Result format covering: a conflict, a failure, a tight deadline, an unpopular decision, a process you improved, and your best win. Your clearance, audit, or oversight war stories are more interesting than you think — told in plain English.

The short version

  • ✓Do tailor the top third of page one to each posting — that's all most screeners read.
  • ✓Do include a 3-line summary that names your target role, not your federal history.
  • ✓Do have a non-government friend read it: every acronym they stumble on, translate or cut.
  • ✗Don't submit your USAJOBS resume to a private employer — ever.
  • ✗Don't list every duty; list what changed because you did the job.
  • ✗Don't undersell oversight experience — regulated industries pay for exactly that.
Educational, not advice: This guide explains general rules that change and have exceptions. Verify current figures with OPM, TSP.gov, and IRS guidance, and consult a licensed professional about your specific situation before acting.
