FERS After You Leave: Deferred vs. Postponed Retirement, Explained

If you leave government before full retirement eligibility, you don't necessarily walk away from your pension. Two paths — deferred and postponed — sound similar and are wildly different where it hurts: health insurance.

First: are you vested?

With 5 years of creditable civilian service, you're vested in FERS. Leave your contributions in the system and you have a future annuity waiting. Take a refund of your contributions instead, and you erase that service — a decision that's expensive to undo if you ever return to government.

Deferred retirement: for those who leave before MRA

Leave with 5+ years but before your Minimum Retirement Age (57 for anyone born in 1970 or later), and you can claim a deferred annuity at 62 (or at MRA with 20+ years, with reductions). The formula is the same 1% × high-3 × years you'd get otherwise — frozen at your separation date, with no COLAs until it starts.

The catch that surprises everyone: deferred retirees permanently lose FEHB and FEGLI. Your federal health insurance does not come back at 62.

Postponed retirement: the MRA+10 escape hatch

If you've already reached your MRA with at least 10 years of service when you leave, you qualify for MRA+10 retirement — but taking it immediately means a 5%-per-year reduction for every year under 62. Postponing the start avoids the reduction.

Here's why the distinction matters: postponed retirees can REINSTATE FEHB when the annuity begins (if you were enrolled your last 5 years of service). Deferred retirees cannot. If you're within striking distance of your MRA, the difference between leaving a month early and a month late can be lifetime health coverage.

The FERS supplement and other details

The FERS annuity supplement (bridging Social Security until 62) is generally not payable under deferred or MRA+10 postponed retirement — plan your income bridge accordingly. And your unused sick leave only counts if you retire on an immediate annuity, not a deferred one.

The short version

  • ✓Do get your official service history (SF-50s) and verify your creditable service before you separate.
  • ✓Do check your MRA date against your separation date — sometimes waiting weeks changes everything.
  • ✓Do keep FEHB coverage in your last 5 years if there's any chance you'll want it back in retirement.
  • ✗Don't take a FERS contribution refund without pricing what you're giving up.
  • ✗Don't assume FEHB follows you — under deferred retirement, it's gone for good.
  • ✗Don't confuse deferred (annuity claimable later, no FEHB) with postponed (MRA+10, FEHB restorable).
